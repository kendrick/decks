A case study

  • MRI equipment was nominated for an international industrial design award
  • But after watching it in use with a certain group of patients, designer began to see some flaws
  • Claustrophobic. Loud noises. It’s in cramped, dark rooms. Terrifying experience if you’re a kid who needs an MRI.
  • Pediatric patients were so scared of these conditions that they couldn’t lie still enough to run the test.
  • Around 80% had to be sedated for their MRI.

80% of patients had to be sedated.

“I failed at my job with the first machine.”

So the designer went back to the drawing board, redesigning what was already an acclaimed, commercially successful product.

  • No support from GE
  • User research: kids at daycare, pediatric doctors, experts at children's museums
  • Huge amount of empathy that completely transformed the experience
  • "Let's board the submarine and see what creatures we can find at the bottom of the ocean! But hold still—we don't want to scare them."

Sedation rates dropped from 80% to 0.01%.

Sedation was addressing the symptom, not the root problem.
